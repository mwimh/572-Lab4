Wildfire Risk to Rural Infrastructure

The intention of this project was to analyze the western United States to find infrastructure clusters outside of urban areas, then evaulate the risk of wildfires to these clusters based on current climate data and predicted future climate data. Significant clusters of infrastructure exist outside of urban areas and if damaged by wildfire, can have significant effects on a region or the entire coutry. Transportation, power generation, and petroleum product infrastructure was evaluated to find clusters and fuel availability and climate factors were evaluated to determine wildfire risk. The wildfire risk and infrastructure clusters were overlaid to determine which clusters were most at risk of wildfire both now and in the future.
